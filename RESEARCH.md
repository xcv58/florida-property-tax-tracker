# Research Workflow

## Priority Order For Sources

1. Florida Senate official bill pages.
2. Florida House official bill pages.
3. Official bill text PDFs.
4. Committee agendas and packets.
5. Governor special session proclamations.
6. Florida Department of State ballot records.
7. Revenue Estimating Conference and EDR fiscal materials.
8. County government materials.
9. Stakeholder materials.
10. News and analysis.

## What To Check During Active Periods

- New HJR/SJR filings.
- New special session proclamations.
- Committee agendas.
- Committee votes.
- Floor calendars.
- Vote histories.
- Amendments.
- Bill text changes.
- Fiscal-impact documents.
- Department of State ballot postings.
- County impact reports.
- Public statements from major officials.

## How To Handle Source Conflicts

If official sources conflict with news reports, use official sources for official status and news only for context or quotes.

If stakeholder sources conflict, label each source and do not choose a side unless official data resolves the conflict.

If bill text changed, preserve the older version, add a new version entry, update claims tied to that version, and warn when older analyses may describe older text.

## How To Write Summaries

Use plain English, short sentences, exact dates, and source-backed claims. Avoid speculation, partisan framing, unsourced predictions, and vague phrases like "recently" without a date.
